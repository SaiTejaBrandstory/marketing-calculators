Customer Purchase Probability Model

The Customer Purchase Probability Model helps businesses predict the likelihood of a customer making a purchase based on key attributes like engagement level, past purchase history, and interactions with marketing campaigns. Enter the necessary data to calculate the probability of a customer making a purchase.

Formula:

The formula for purchase probability estimation is:

Purchase Probability = (Engagement Score * 0.4) + (Purchase History * 0.3) + (Marketing Interaction * 0.3)

Example Calculation:

If the values are:

  • Engagement Score: 80
  • Purchase History: 60
  • Marketing Interaction: 70

The purchase probability would be:

((80 * 0.4) + (60 * 0.3) + (70 * 0.3)) = 71%

Input for Customer Purchase Probability Model

Engagement Score (0-100)
Purchase History (0-100)
Marketing Interaction (0-100)
Purchase Probability (%)

71%

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

The Customer Purchase Probability Model predicts the likelihood that a customer will make a purchase based on various factors, including their engagement with the brand, purchase history, and marketing interactions.

The purchase probability is calculated using a weighted sum of three factors: engagement score, purchase history, and marketing interaction, with different weights assigned to each factor.

Understanding the customer purchase probability helps businesses tailor their marketing strategies and focus on customers with a higher chance of conversion, improving ROI.
