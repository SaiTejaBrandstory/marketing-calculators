SEO Experiment Significance Calculator

The SEO Experiment Significance Calculator helps assess the significance of your SEO experiments by evaluating metrics such as clicks, impressions, and conversion rates. This allows you to understand if the changes you've made to your SEO strategy have a statistically significant impact.

Formula:

Significance Score = (New Metric - Old Metric) / Standard Deviation

Example Calculation:
  • Old Metric (e.g., Clicks): 1000
  • New Metric (e.g., Clicks): 1200
  • Standard Deviation: 100
  • Significance Score: (1200 - 1000) / 100 = 2.00

FAQ

This calculator evaluates the statistical significance of changes in SEO metrics like clicks, impressions, or conversion rates after implementing SEO changes to your site.

A higher significance score indicates a stronger statistical difference between the old and new metrics. Generally, a score above 2 is considered statistically significant.

The standard deviation is a measure of the variability of your data. A higher standard deviation means greater variability, and the significance score is adjusted accordingly.
