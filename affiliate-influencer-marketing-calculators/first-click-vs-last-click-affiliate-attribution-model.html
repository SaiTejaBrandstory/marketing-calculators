First-Click vs. Last-Click Affiliate Attribution Model

This model helps compare the First-Click and Last-Click attribution methods used in affiliate marketing. Understanding these models is important for determining how affiliate contributions are credited in the conversion process.

First-Click Attribution Model:

In the First-Click Attribution Model, the first affiliate or touchpoint that a user interacts with before making a purchase is credited with the conversion. This model is used to understand which initial interactions are driving customer interest.

Last-Click Attribution Model:

In the Last-Click Attribution Model, the affiliate or touchpoint that directly results in the final conversion (i.e., the last click) is credited with the sale. This model is more common in determining the final actions that led to the conversion.
